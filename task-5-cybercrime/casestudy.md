# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

In 2024, a college student named Abhinav Sharma from Indore became a victim of a UPI payment fraud. One evening, he received a phone call from a person claiming to be a customer care executive from a well-known digital payment platform like Google Pay. The caller informed Abhinav that his account had a “security issue” and needed immediate verification to avoid being blocked.

Trusting the caller, Abhinav was asked to install a screen-sharing app and follow certain instructions. The fraudster then guided him to approve a “collect request” on his UPI app, falsely stating that it was for verification purposes. Without realizing, Abhinav entered his UPI PIN, which actually authorized a payment instead of receiving money.

Within minutes, ₹25,000 was debited from his bank account. The fraudster disconnected the call and became unreachable. Abhinav later realized that UPI apps never ask users to enter a PIN to receive money and that he had been tricked into sending money instead.

He immediately reported the incident to his bank and filed a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Portal. However, due to the delay in reporting, only a small portion of the money could be recovered.

Conclusion:
This case highlights how social engineering and lack of awareness can lead to financial loss. Users must remember that no legitimate service will ask for a UPI PIN or request screen sharing. Awareness and caution are the best defenses against such cyber frauds.
